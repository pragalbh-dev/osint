Unit History and Lineage Notes — Reference Series

11 Signal Regiment

Compiled for internal training reference use. Not for public release.

Formation and Role

11 Signal Regiment is a British Army signals formation currently based at Blandford Camp, Dorset, and administratively grouped under 1st (United Kingdom) Signal Brigade. The regiment is primarily tasked with the provision of trained instructional and demonstration support to the Royal School of Signals, alongside limited operational communications support tasking as required by Army Headquarters. Its subordinate squadrons rotate periodically through instructional postings, and manning levels are believed to be in the region of 450–550 all ranks, though exact figures fluctuate with the training cycle and are not published in open sources.

Lineage

The regiment's lineage traces to a series of wartime and post-war signal units that were amalgamated and redesignated on several occasions between 1944 and the mid-1960s. Elements of the present-day regiment reportedly carry forward battle honours associated with signals support to armoured formations during the North West Europe campaign, though the precise chain of redesignations before 1959 is not consistently recorded across regimental histories, and some accounts differ on the exact date of the final consolidation.

Current Organisation (as of September 2020)

- Regimental Headquarters — Blandford Camp
- 3 (or possibly 4, sources vary) sub-units, each notionally squadron-sized, tasked with training support, trials assistance, and demonstration duties for courses run by the Royal School of Signals
- A small headquarters support element providing administrative and welfare functions

The regiment does not maintain a standing operational deployment posture comparable to line signal regiments, though personnel are periodically attached to deployed formations on an individual augmentee basis. Equipment holdings are understood to be oriented toward training aids and legacy communications systems retained for instructional purposes, supplemented by current-issue Bowman variants for familiarisation training. Specific equipment scales are not detailed in this reference note.

Notable Activities, 2019–2020

Open reporting indicates the regiment supported several exercises during the training year, including contributions to command post exercises run out of the Royal School of Signals and periodic support to officer and NCO promotion courses. A regimental open day, reportedly held annually in the summer, was understood to have been curtailed or reformatted in 2020 owing to public health restrictions; no further detail is available in this collection.

Affiliations

The regiment maintains affiliations with several Royal Corps of Signals associations and cadet units, the exact number and current status of which is not verified in this note. Ceremonial duties are occasional and typically limited to regimental or corps-level events at Blandford.

Sourcing

This entry draws on publicly available regimental association newsletters, army list extracts, and general reference compilations current to September 2020. Some details, particularly regarding exact unit strength and precise lineage dates, could not be corroborated across all consulted sources and should be treated as indicative rather than definitive.

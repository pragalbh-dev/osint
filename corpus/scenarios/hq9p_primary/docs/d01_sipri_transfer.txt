--- narrative ---

Continuing coverage of the PAF's long-range SAM layer: three independent defence-press items this cycle (two South Asian, one Gulf-based aggregator) reiterate that the HQ-9/P battery order placed with China several years prior has now been delivered and, per one item, "declared operational" along a forward air-defence belt. None of the three outlets sight a contract document; all trace back, directly or indirectly, to the SIPRI Arms Transfers Database's running entry for this transfer, which analysts continue to treat as the reference tracker of record even though the underlying TIV line was not independently re-verified for this issue.

One contributor forwarded a translated procurement-adjacent trade note (origin: regional customs-adjacent bulletin, unverified provenance) that put a rough figure on the deal — see comment thread appended below — but the figure could not be cross-checked against any customs schedule, since long-range SAM systems of this class typically move under end-user-certificate G2G channels rather than commercial HS-coded freight, and no bill of lading or tariff line was located for this row.

Analyst note appended to row: treat unit count and battery number as indicative only pending a cleaner primary sighting; the "HQ-9/P" designator itself is the exporter's export-market naming and should not be conflated with the PLA's domestic HQ-9 baseline or the shipborne HHQ-9 variant, which are unrelated to this transfer.

PAKISTAN — PUNJAB AIR DEFENCE BELT

Rahwali airfield (Pakistan Army/PAF joint-use facility, ~10 km NW of Gujranwala along the GT Road corridor, Punjab): commercial EO imagery collected 27 March (Planet SkySat, 0.5m) shows continuity of the TEL and battery-command cluster first flagged in our 14 Feb issue in the northeast dispersal area. Six TEL-pattern vehicles remain in a fan arrangement consistent with prior HQ-9B associated deployments; a large octagonal engagement radar signature, believed to be the HT-233 fire-control/engagement radar associated with the HQ-9B system, is again visible on its prepared hardstand roughly 380 m east of the TEL cluster — position essentially unchanged from the 14 Feb pass, suggesting either a settled emplacement or a decoy array maintained for persistence.

Of note this cycle: a second, non-imagery indicator has closed some of the ambiguity around the site. A commercial ELINT aggregator feed (operator not disclosed to subscribers, relayed via a third-party maritime/air RF-monitoring service) logged an emitter-on fix in the same general locale during the reporting window consistent with HT-233-band engagement-radar parameters; the fix window is described by the vendor only as "high confidence, single pass" with no further geolocation detail released at this subscription tier. Separately, a repeat optical pass on 29 March (different collection platform than the 27 March pass) again shows the radar hardstand occupied and TEL count unchanged. Taken together, an imagery-only assessment carried real decoy risk given the PLA/PAF's documented use of inflatable and radar-reflective decoy TELs at dispersal sites elsewhere in the region; a non-imagery emitter fix landing on the same location in the same window is not something a static decoy layout would produce on its own. Analysts should still treat the HT-233 identification itself as the vendor's characterization, not independently re-derived by this desk.

Order-of-battle context: Rahwali has been associated in open reporting with a PAF/Army Air Defence Command HQ-9BE (export variant, sometimes rendered HQ-9P in Pakistani service literature) battery since at least 2021, reportedly one of the initial units re-equipped after the type's induction was first reported around 2021–22. Battery strength at this site is estimated at 6–8 TELs plus one engagement radar and associated command/guidance van, though the desk's confidence in the "8" upper bound remains low given intermittent TEL visibility across passes attributable to routine maintenance rotation, camouflage netting, or genuine redeployment — no reporting available this cycle to distinguish among these.

No new activity to report at the previously tracked Sialkot-area and Pasrur dispersal sites this period; both remain in a "last confirmed" state from the 07 March pass.

Cross-reference: readers tracking the HQ-9/HQ-9B/HQ-9BE/HQ-9P designator family should note continuing inconsistency across open sources in how Pakistani service designators map to the PLA domestic HQ-9B baseline versus the CASIC export HQ-9BE marketing designation; this digest uses "HQ-9B" generically per prior issues unless a source specifies otherwise.

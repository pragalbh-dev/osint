SUBJECT: Probable Long-Range SAM Emplacement, Malir District, Karachi, Sindh Province, Pakistan

DATE OF REPORT: 2022-03-14
PERIOD OF IMAGERY COVERAGE: 2021-11-02 through 2022-03-09 (multi-pass, 4 collects)

1. SUMMARY

Analysis of commercially available satellite imagery over the Malir area on the northeastern periphery of Karachi has identified a fortified air defense site exhibiting a petal/ring-pattern surface-to-air missile pad consistent with known HQ-9/P (export variant of the CASIC HQ-9, NATO CH-SA-9) transporter-erector-launcher (TEL) deployment schemes. The site is assessed with MODERATE-HIGH confidence to be an active or newly constructed long-range SAM battery position. Coordinate pin, derived from multi-pass geolocation and cross-referenced against basemap control points, is:

24.9012 N, 67.2034 E

(decimal degrees, WGS-84; analyst-plotted centroid of central hardstand)

2. IMAGERY DISCUSSION

Pass 1 (2021-11-02, ~0.5m resolution, off-nadir ~12°): initial tip from a change-detection tile flagged new earthworks in an agricultural/scrubland belt east of the Malir Cantonment road network. A roughly circular graded pad, approximate diameter 90-110m, is visible with six radial spurs terminating in what are believed to be TEL revetments — earthen berms 2-3m in estimated height, open-topped, oriented outward from a central hub. This "petal" or "flower" layout is a signature associated with HQ-9/P and analogous S-300/400-family battery construction, intended to provide blast separation between launcher vehicles and a central engagement/guidance radar position.

Pass 2 (2021-12-19): revetment construction appears more complete; a raised circular foundation at pad center, approx. 15-20m diameter, is consistent with an emplaced engagement radar (possibly HT-233 or derivative, NATO association tentative). No radar array itself is resolvable at this resolution/sun angle — presence inferred from foundation geometry alone.

Pass 3 (2022-01-27): shadow analysis suggests object(s) present in at least two of the six revetments, elongated, approx. 9-11m in length — dimensions broadly compatible with an HQ-9/P TEL (reported ~8m missile canister length, chassis variable), though a shorter-range system or unrelated support vehicle cannot be excluded at this resolution. Object count is uncertain; possible additional occupied revetment obscured by cloud shadow along the northern arc.

Pass 4 (2022-03-09): pad and revetment layout unchanged from Pass 3. Perimeter berm and what is assessed as a secondary access road (unpaved, ~6m width) connecting to the main Malir-Gadap corridor are now clearly resolved. No additional radar or support infrastructure (fire control van, generator trailers) positively identified, though several small rectangular objects near the pad's southern edge may represent support shelters or unrelated agricultural structures.

3. ASSESSMENT

The petal/ring pad geometry, revetment count, and TEL-consistent object dimensions are judged sufficient to assess this site as a likely HQ-9/P battery position, reportedly part of Pakistan Army Air Defence Command's continuing long-range SAM buildout. Confidence in system-type identification (HQ-9/P specifically, vs. a legacy or alternate long-range SAM) is MODERATE; no hull markings, canister markings, or radar signatures were collectable from available commercial imagery. The site's proximity to Karachi's urban/port periphery is consistent with previously reported point-defense deployments oriented toward coastal and airspace approaches, though the analyst notes this specific location has not, to this analyst's knowledge, previously appeared in open literature.

Estimated operational status: possibly not yet fully operational as of the March pass — absence of a resolvable radar array is the principal open question. Object count within revetments (2 confirmed, up to 4 possible) should be treated as provisional pending a higher-resolution or more favorable sun-angle collect.

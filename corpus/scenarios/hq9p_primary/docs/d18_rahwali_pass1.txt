1. SUMMARY

A single commercial electro-optical satellite pass tasked over Rahwali airfield (Gujranwala Cantonment area), Punjab, Pakistan, shows an array consistent with a Pakistan Army Air Defence Command long-range SAM deployment. Configuration is assessed as likely HQ-9B (export designator; PLA domestic HQ-9/HQ-9A lineage, CASIC), based on radar/launcher spacing and revetment layout compared against reference signatures. Assessment is SINGLE-SOURCE, SINGLE-PASS — no repeat collection or corroborating discipline (SIGINT/COMINT, additional EO/SAR tasking) has been obtained as of this writing.

2. LOCATION

Center of signature: 32°14′20″N 074°07′52″E (WGS-84, derived from image metadata + manual pixel measurement against basemap; estimated CE90 ~15-20m, not independently surveyed).

Site sits on the northern perimeter apron/dispersal area associated with Rahwali airfield, approx. 3.8 km NNE of Gujranwala city center by rough measurement. No prior open-source reporting located that specifically identifies this dispersal pad as a SAM deployment area; PAF/Army Air Defence order-of-battle references for Rahwali are limited in open literature and largely inferential.

- No berm/revetment maturity assessment possible from single pass — vegetation/shadow state suggests position may be recently occupied, though this is a soft inference from one image and could reflect routine exercise deployment rather than a fixed emplacement.

4. ANALYST COMMENTS

This is treated internally as a candidate signature only. Confidence in system-type ID is MODERATE at best given the single-pass, single-discipline nature of the collection; confidence in "operational SAM site" vs. "transient exercise/training deployment" is LOW-MODERATE.

Rawalpindi – May 12, 2025

Air Defence Command carries out routine training activity

As part of the Pakistan Army's regular annual training calendar, Army Air Defence Command today conducted a scheduled training activity involving long-range air defence assets in the central air defence sector. The activity, planned well in advance under the current training cycle, was aimed at evaluating crew proficiency, system readiness and standard operating procedures of deployed air defence units.

The exercise involved routine deployment and repositioning of ground-based air defence elements, radar activation drills, and simulated engagement sequences conducted strictly within a designated and pre-notified training area. All activities were carried out in accordance with standard peacetime training protocols and applicable air-space coordination procedures.

Troops and technical crews of Air Defence Command displayed a high level of professionalism during the drills. The Air Officer Commanding of the concerned Air Defence Group appreciated the training standards and operational readiness of participating units and reiterated that such periodic exercises are a routine and continuing feature of the armed forces' training regime, aimed solely at maintaining defensive preparedness.

It is reiterated that the training activity was of a routine nature, planned within the normal training cycle, and carries no bearing on the regional security situation. Reports circulating on certain social media platforms alluding to unusual troop or equipment movement in the area are speculative and not reflective of facts on the ground.

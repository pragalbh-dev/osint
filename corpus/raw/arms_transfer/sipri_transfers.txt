TRANSFER 1 of 2 — China -> Pakistan, HQ-9/P (HQ-9BE) SAM system
================================================================================

--- ROW 1 (launcher / system) -------------------------------------------------
Recipient             : Pakistan
Supplier               : China
No. ordered            : [unverified] — neither government has publicly
                          disclosed contract quantities. Quwa (2026): "Pakistan
                          has not publicly disclosed the exact number of HQ-9
                          batteries in service... The exact regiment and
                          battery count remains classified." One open-source
                          blog (trishul-trident.blogspot.com, Oct 2021, NOT
                          independently corroborated) claims 2 Army batteries
                          x 4 TELs = 8 launchers — reproduced here only as a
                          flagged, unconfirmed data point, not a verified figure.
Weapon designation      : HQ-9/P (Pakistan Army variant); HQ-9BE (Pakistan Air
                          Force extended-range variant). CASIC's generic export
                          designation for the underlying system is FD-2000
                          (NOT the same system as the FT-2000 anti-radiation
                          variant — a known false-merge in some trackers, e.g.
                          FAS). Chinese domestic designation: HQ-9 / Hongqi-9
                          (红旗-9).
Weapon description      : Mobile long-range/high-to-medium-altitude SAM system.
                          4-round transporter-erector-launcher (TEL) per Quwa's
                          spec table; HT-233 target-acquisition/engagement
                          radar (FD-2000 export baseline) + Type 120
                          low-altitude search radar + Type 305A AESA search
                          radar (Quwa); optional YLC-20 passive radar for
                          stealth-aircraft detection. Pakistan Army HQ-9/P:
                          ~125 km vs aircraft, ~25 km vs cruise missiles.
                          Pakistan Air Force HQ-9BE: 260-280 km, active
                          radar-homing (ARH) terminal guidance (Quwa).
Year of order           : [unverified] — Wikipedia/Quwa both state
                          "negotiations for the purchase of the HQ-9 and HQ-16
                          by Pakistan began in early 2015," alongside HQ-16
                          talks; no publicly disclosed contract-signature date
                          found for the HQ-9/HQ-9BE side of that negotiation.
Year(s) of delivery     : [unverified as physical-delivery dates] — the only
                          firmly dated public milestones are IN-SERVICE /
                          OPERATIONAL announcements, not shipment dates:
                            - Pakistan Army HQ-9/P: publicly commissioned/
                              inducted 14 Oct 2021 (ISPR press release, Army
                              Air Defence Centre, Karachi).
                            - Pakistan Air Force HQ-9BE: reported operational
                              by 19 Aug 2025 (Global Defense Corp); no more
                              precise delivery year located in sources
                              reviewed.
No. delivered/produced  : [unverified] — see No. ordered note above; same
                          "2 batteries" blog claim applies, same caveat.
Comments                : ISPR communique, 14 Oct 2021, quoted directly by
                          Army Recognition ("the official communique read")
                          and corroborated by multiple secondary outlets:
                          "Induction of HIMADS will significantly enhance
                          Comprehensive Layered Integrated Air Defence
                          (CLIAD) shield of aerial frontiers of Pakistan as
                          the system is fully integrated through a well-knit
                          Digitised System on its inventory." / "Capable of
                          intercepting multiple air targets including
                          aircrafts, cruise missiles and Beyond Visual Range
                          Weapons at ranges over 100 kilometers with Single
                          Shot Kill Probability, HQ-9/P is considered as a
                          strategic long range Surface to Air Missile (SAM)."
                          Ceremony attended by COAS Gen Qamar Javed Bajwa;
                          Pakistan is the first HQ-9 operator outside China
                          (Quwa). Both HQ-9/P and HQ-9BE were combat-employed
                          in the May 2025 India-Pakistan conflict — the first
                          confirmed combat use of any HQ-9 variant (Quwa,
                          Wikipedia, multiple trade press). Local service
                          nickname: HIMADS (High-to-Medium Air Defence
                          System). SIPRI is cited by Quwa as having "tracked
                          Chinese SAM imports and technology transfers
                          extensively in its arms transfer database," but the
                          live DB entry itself was not accessible this
                          session (see provenance header) — treat all
                          quantities above as pending re-verification.

TRANSFER 2 of 2 — Russia -> China, S-400 Triumf (SA-21 Growler) SAM system
================================================================================

--- ROW 1 (launcher / system) -------------------------------------------------
Recipient              : China
Supplier                : Russia
No. ordered             : Widely reported as 6 battalions (~3 regimental sets)
                          — CSIS Missile Threat ("In 2015, China signed an
                          agreement with Russia to purchase six battalions of
                          the system"); Army Recognition 2015 headline
                          ("Russia has sold six battalions of S-400... to
                          China"); Erickson/Jane's-Reuters summary (13 Apr
                          2015) reports "4-6 battalions." CONFLICTING REPORT:
                          TASS (Dec 2019/Jan 2020, citing an unnamed
                          "military-diplomatic source," not officially
                          confirmed) frames the original 2014 agreement as
                          covering "two regimental sets of S-400" (each
                          described by TASS as "consisting of two divisions
                          [divizions/battalions] of launch devices..." i.e.
                          ~4 battalions total) — this does not reconcile
                          cleanly with the "6 battalions" figure above.
                          [unverified] which figure/unit SIPRI itself codes
                          in "No. ordered" for this row.
Weapon designation       : S-400 Triumf (Russian: С-400 Триумф; NATO reporting
                          name SA-21 Growler); formerly S-300PMU-3.
Weapon description       : Mobile long-range/high-altitude SAM system. TELs:
                          5P85TE2 (self-propelled) / 5P85SE2 (trailer, towed
                          by BAZ-64022/MAZ-543M); radars: 91N6E acquisition
                          ("Big Bird", ~340 km declared range per Wikipedia),
                          92N6E multi-function engagement radar, 96L6E
                          detector; C2: 55K6E command post under the 30K6E
                          administration system. Longest-range interceptor
                          (40N6) purported range ~400 km (Wikipedia).
Year of order            : 2014 — Russian President Putin authorized the sale
                          in March 2014 (Wikipedia); contract publicly
                          confirmed by the Rosoboronexport CEO on 13 Apr 2015
                          (Erickson, citing Reuters/Jane's; Wikipedia gives
                          contract-signing as "September 2014"). Estimated
                          value ~US$3 billion (Army Recognition 2015;
                          Erickson/Jane's-Reuters; Airforce Technology 2015).
Year(s) of delivery      : 2018-2019 confirmed for the first two regimental
                          sets:
                            - 1st regimental set: deliveries began Jan 2018,
                              completed/handover ~May 2018 (The Diplomat,
                              Apr/May/Jul 2018 pieces; China test-fired the
                              system in early Aug 2018).
                            - 2nd regimental set: delivery began ~Jul 2019,
                              handover certificate reportedly signed in China
                              Dec 2019 (The Diplomat Feb 2020; TASS, citing an
                              unconfirmed military-diplomatic source).
                          Status of any further regimental sets beyond these
                          two: [unverified] — Wikipedia states "Deliveries
                          were reportedly postponed after the delivery of two
                          S-400 units" and no source reviewed this session
                          confirms delivery of a 3rd set through 2026.
No. delivered/produced   : 2 regimental sets (~4 battalions) confirmed
                          delivered by early 2020 per the sources above.
                          Delivery status of the remainder of the original
                          order (if the true order was 6 battalions rather
                          than the ~4 TASS describes) is [unverified].
Comments                 : Deal negotiated/announced as government-to-
                          government (Rosoboronexport); first foreign S-400
                          sale, ahead of Turkey (2017) and India (2018).
                          Strategic rationale reported: extends coverage over
                          the Taiwan Strait and Senkaku/Diaoyu Islands
                          dispute area (Wikipedia, citing contemporary
                          reporting). China test-fired the system for the
                          first time in early August 2018 (Wikipedia). SIPRI
                          Arms Transfers Database is the standard citation
                          for this deal across the secondary literature
                          (CSIS Missile Threat, RUSI Bronk 2020, multiple
                          trade press) but its row-level entry could not be
                          queried directly this session (auth-gated backend —
                          see provenance header).

INSTITUTE 214 (NO. 6 RESEARCH INSTITUTE, CHINA ELECTRONICS TECHNOLOGY GROUP CORPORATION)

INTERNAL BULLETIN — PARTY COMMITTEE OFFICE / OFFICE OF SCIENTIFIC RESEARCH MANAGEMENT

No. 2019-06 (June Issue)

Internal distribution only. Not for external release.

I. PERSONNEL MATTERS

1. Comrade Zhang Weiguo (张伟国), formerly Deputy Chief Engineer of the Vacuum Microwave Devices Laboratory, has been appointed Chief Engineer of the Institute's Second Research Division, effective 1 June 2019. Comrade Zhang has led work on traveling-wave tube cathode assemblies since 2011 and will continue to oversee the reliability testing group in an advisory capacity through the transition period.

2. Ms. Chen Lifang (陈丽芳, also rendered in prior bulletins as Chen Li-fang) has completed her secondment to the Institute's Guilin branch and returned to the Hefei campus to resume duties in the Ceramic Materials Section. Her secondment report on alumina substrate yield improvements is under internal review by the Technical Committee and is expected to be circulated to relevant sections by the end of Q3.

3. Retirement: Senior Engineer Liu Baosheng, associated with the Institute's frequency-source group since 1988, retired on 30 May 2019 after 31 years of service. A farewell gathering was held in the Building 3 conference hall on 28 May, attended by an estimated 40–50 staff and retirees.

4. New hires: Six graduates from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC) and three from Hefei University of Technology have joined the Institute's postgraduate training program this quarter, assigned across the Microwave Devices, Signal Processing, and Materials Science sections.

II. FACILITIES AND INFRASTRUCTURE

1. Renovation of the West Campus vacuum-tube pilot production line (Workshop 4) is reportedly nearing completion, with commissioning of the new ion-pumping stations expected in July 2019. The workshop's floor space has been expanded by approximately 1,200 square meters to accommodate additional clean-room bays for cathode assembly.

2. The Institute's telecommunications components testing laboratory has procured a new set of vector network analyzers (believed to be Keysight-sourced, procurement finalized in April) to support ongoing work on satellite ground-station amplifier modules for civilian communications customers.

3. Dormitory Building 7, housing approximately 200 junior researchers and support staff, has completed its scheduled maintenance cycle, including replacement of elevator systems and upgrades to the building's water supply infrastructure.

III. RESEARCH AND ACADEMIC ACTIVITIES

1. The Institute co-hosted, jointly with the Hefei branch of the Chinese Institute of Electronics, a two-day symposium on "Vacuum Electron Device Applications in Broadband Communications" held 14–15 May 2019 at the Anhui Hotel, Hefei. Attendance was estimated at 180 participants from academic and industrial units nationwide, including delegations reportedly from Chengdu, Nanjing, and Shanghai research institutes.

2. Three papers authored by Institute researchers were accepted for the 2019 International Vacuum Electronics Conference (IVEC), covering topics in high-power klystron cathode longevity, traveling-wave tube efficiency improvements for satellite communication payloads, and materials degradation in long-life microwave tube components.

3. The Institute's technical journal, internally circulated as "Vacuum Electronics Technology Bulletin," published its second 2019 issue in early June, featuring an overview of the Institute's collaboration with a domestic telecommunications equipment manufacturer (name withheld pending contract finalization) on ground-station amplifier modules for rural broadband deployment.

IV. ADMINISTRATIVE NOTICES

1. Staff are reminded that the annual health examination for employees over 45 years of age will be conducted at the Institute clinic between 17–28 June 2019. Sign-up sheets are available at each section office.

2. The Institute's canteen renovation, delayed from its original April completion target, is now expected to conclude by mid-July. Staff are advised to continue using the temporary dining facility adjacent to Workshop 2.

3. Submissions for the third-quarter "Advanced Worker" recognition nominations should be submitted to the Party Committee Office no later than 20 June 2019. Approximately 30 nominations were received in the previous quarter, of which 12 were approved.

V. MISCELLANEOUS

The Institute's basketball team placed second in the CETC regional inter-institute tournament held in Wuhu, Anhui, in late May, losing the final match to a team fielded by a sister institute in the Nanjing area.

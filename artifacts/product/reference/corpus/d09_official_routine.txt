ISPR PRESS RELEASE

No PR-XX/2025-ISPR
Rawalpindi — 9 May 2025

ROUTINE AIR DEFENCE TRAINING ACTIVITY — SINDH

1. As part of Pakistan Army Air Defence Command's periodic training calendar, a scheduled training activity involving long-range air defence assets was conducted today, 09 May 2025, in the general area east of Karachi.

2. The activity forms part of routine seasonal exercises undertaken to maintain crew proficiency and system readiness, and is not linked to any operational deployment or change in the region's security posture. No unit movement beyond the designated training area was undertaken.

3. Reports circulating on social media regarding "large-scale mobilisation" or "activation" of air defence systems in the Karachi area are speculative and not reflective of facts on the ground. Such training serials are conducted periodically and in accordance with standard operating procedures.

4. All activity was carried out within a controlled and pre-notified training zone, in coordination with civil aviation authorities. There is no cause for public concern.

-ISPR-

Jane's Defence Weekly — Asia-Pacific Desk
"Imagery indicates possible SAM battery repositioning near Karachi"
09 May 2025 (web), by staff correspondent

Commercial satellite imagery captured on the morning of 9 May 2025 and reviewed by Jane's shows what is assessed with moderate confidence to be transporter-erector-launcher (TEL) activity at a site previously associated with a Pakistan Army Air Defence Command HQ-9/P (export variant of the Chinese HQ-9, reportedly locally designated in some PAF/PA literature simply as the "HQ-9P" or "FD-2000"-derived system) battalion, roughly 30-35 km east-northeast of Karachi.

The imagery, timestamped 0947 local, reportedly shows between three and five (estimate uncertain due to cloud cover and resolution) wheeled TEL-type vehicles dispersed away from what appears to be a known garrison/storage compound, along with what may be an engagement radar, believed to be an HT-233-series or derivative, in an unstowed configuration. Analysts caution that vehicle count and radar identification cannot be fully confirmed from a single overhead pass.

A separate ground source, speaking on condition of anonymity and described as "familiar with regional army logistics," told Jane's that the movement is consistent with a battery-level relocation rather than a static training serial, though this could not be independently corroborated.

This activity, if confirmed, would be notable given recent regional tensions, though Jane's stresses that Pakistani training cycles for long-range SAM units have historically included similar dispersal patterns without operational significance. ISPR did not respond to a request for comment prior to publication; a same-day ISPR release characterized unrelated air defence activity in the Karachi area as "routine training."

Jane's continues to monitor the site and will update this assessment as further imagery becomes available. Confidence level: LOW-MODERATE.

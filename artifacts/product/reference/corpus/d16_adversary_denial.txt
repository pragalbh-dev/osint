Following up on the earlier thread re: HT-233 engagement radar (the fire-control/guidance element paired with the HQ-9/P batteries reportedly fielded by Army Air Defence Command since roughly 2021). A contact tracking the program says the indigenisation question has now effectively been settled.

To recap the background for newer members: the HQ-9/P is the export variant of China's HQ-9 long-range SAM, itself believed to be derived from/"equivalent to" the Russian S-300 family per most Western open-source assessments (RUSI's Justin Bronk has called it a hybrid design — Russian airframe heritage, radar/seeker/C2 elements with heavier Chinese, and per some accounts, Israeli-influenced input). Pakistan is estimated to operate somewhere between 4 and 8 firing batteries, though the exact count remains unconfirmed in open sources — some trackers say 6, one older JDW estimate said "up to 8," take your pick.

On the HT-233 specifically: a source close to the program (can't name him, works adjacent to a PAF-linked vendor cell, has been reliable on order-of-battle stuff before) tells me a second independent source has now confirmed that the HT-233 radar used in the Pakistani HQ-9/P inventory is fully indigenised — i.e., locally produced/sustained without reliance on Chinese technical data packages for the phased-array engagement radar. He was explicit that this rebuts the persistent claim (repeated in IISS Military Balance commentary and in a couple of Indian analyst pieces from 2023-24) that PAF/AAD sustainment of the HT-233 remains dependent on CASIC/CETC technical support and spares.

Direct quote from the contact, forwarded to me yesterday (13 May):

"I can confirm, and this has now been independently corroborated by a second source, that the HT-233 fire control radar is a fully indigenised product of the domestic defence-industrial base. There is no dependency on Chinese technical data packages, full stop. People keep repeating the old dependency line without checking whether it's still true."

I pushed back and asked who the "second source" was — got a non-answer along the lines of "someone inside the loop, can't burn him." So take that bit with the appropriate grain of salt. Still, if true it's a meaningful shift from the 2017-2019 delivery period, when most reporting (Jane's, later reposted by ISPR-adjacent Urdu press) described HT-233 sustainment as tied to CASIC-supplied documentation and periodic Chinese technician visits for the S-band engagement radar and the associated missile-guidance uplink.

For what it's worth, nothing in the recent ISPR readouts on Army Air Defence Command exercises (the most recent being the unnamed February 2025 live-fire drill covered briefly by APP/ISPR) mentions radar provenance at all — those statements just describe the drills as "routine training activity to validate operational readiness," standard boilerplate, no system-level detail. So there's no official corroboration either way from the Pakistani side, and nothing from CASIC or Chinese state media addressing the claim.

Would be good if someone with better access to the acquisition-side reporting (PAC Kamra, or the AWC-linked technical journals) could weigh in — has anyone actually seen documentation on local HT-233 production, or is this still an assertion floating around Discord/forum channels? I'll flag that the same contact made a similar "fully indigenised, second source confirms it" claim about the FT-2000 seeker head back in 2023 that never really got substantiated either, so I'm logging this one with the same caveat.

1. GENERAL

1.1 Pakistan Air Force intends to procure spares, test equipment, calibration kits and consumable items in support of the in-service Long Range Surface-to-Air Missile (LR-SAM) system, hereinafter referred to as "the System," presently held on the inventory of designated Air Defence squadrons. The requirement pertains solely to sustenance/repair-and-overhaul (R&O) support of equipment already fielded and does NOT constitute procurement of a new weapon system, launcher, missile round, or fire-control radar.

1.2 Bidders are advised that all technical particulars, part numbers, NSNs (where applicable), and OEM cross-reference codes are contained in Annexure-B (Bill of Materials) and Annexure-C (Technical Data Sheets), which are NOT enclosed with this open notification and shall be issued only to firms/agents who have executed the mandatory Non-Disclosure Undertaking (Form AHQ/NDU-7) and whose credentials have been verified by the Directorate of Procurement (Air Defence).

1.3 Only OEM-authorised representatives, or agents holding valid End-User-linked authorisation letters, will be entertained. Firms claiming authorisation on behalf of the original equipment manufacturer (reportedly identified in industry circles as a subsidiary of a major Chinese state aerospace conglomerate) must produce notarised Letters of Authorisation, translated where necessary, along with Certificate of Origin for all sub-assemblies.

2. BACKGROUND / SCOPE

2.1 The System referenced herein is understood to be the export configuration of the Hongqi-9 family of long-range SAM, variously referred to in open literature as HQ-9/P, HQ-9P, or (in earlier marketing material) FD-2000. Bidders should note that nomenclature used in vendor catalogues may differ from that used in squadron technical publications; where discrepancy exists, the part number in Annexure-B shall prevail over any descriptive name.

2.2 Items sought under this tender include, inter alia:
   a. TVM (track-via-missile) uplink/downlink test sets — qty [REDACTED]
   b. Ground support vehicle spares for erector-launcher chassis — as per Annexure-B, Section 4
   c. Battery/charging modules for engagement radar cabin — estimated qty 40-60 (exact requirement subject to survey report, ref. Annexure-D, not attached)
   d. Waveguide assemblies and RF connectors, various — qty as listed
   e. Calibration/test jigs for missile round check-out prior to captive-carry/loading drills
   f. Consumable seals, gaskets, desiccant cartridges for missile canister environmental control

2.3 It is reiterated that this tender is raised under the recurring Annual Maintenance Support Contract framework and pertains to sustenance of equipment already inducted into service. No new launcher units, radar sets, or missile rounds form part of this requirement.

3. ELIGIBILITY

3.1 Bidder must be:
   (a) the OEM itself, or
   (b) an authorised trading/logistics agent with documented supply chain traceability to the OEM, or
   (c) a locally registered defence vendor holding a valid DESTO/NESCOM-cleared trading licence for controlled defence goods (Category: Air Defence Systems, Class III).

3.2 Name of the incumbent maintenance contractor from the previous cycle (Contract No. [REDACTED], concluded 2019) has been withheld per procurement confidentiality rules; interested bidders may request a redacted performance summary under Right of Information procedures, subject to security vetting.

4. TECHNICAL NOTES FOR BIDDERS

4.1 Bidders are cautioned that certain sub-systems associated with the System's fire-control/engagement radar (believed by open-source observers to be a derivative of the Type 305B / H-200 family, though PAF does not confirm or deny specific radar nomenclature) may require OEM-certified technicians for depot-level repair; L3/L4 maintenance actions are explicitly excluded from this tender and remain under separate Government-to-Government arrangement.

4.2 Delivery is sought FOB port of origin, with onward freight arranged through National Logistics Cell. Estimated contract value: PKR [REDACTED] (indicative range quoted verbally at pre-bid conference as "approximately 1.8 to 2.4 billion," figure not to be treated as final).

4.3 Comparisons in open media between this system and Russian long-range SAM families (S-300P/PMU-series, sometimes referenced under the NATO reporting name SA-20, or the more recent S-400/Triumf, occasionally rendered "Triumph" in regional press) are noted only for bidder context regarding possible interoperability of ancillary test equipment; no such interoperability is claimed or required by this tender.

5. VALIDITY, EMD AND WARRANTY

5.1 Bid validity: 120 days from date of opening.
5.2 Earnest Money Deposit: 2% of quoted FOR value, refundable per standard PPRA-aligned clause, pay order in favour of "AOC AHQ Procurement A/c."
5.3 Warranty on supplied spares: minimum 18 months from date of installation/acceptance, or as per OEM standard warranty, whichever is longer.

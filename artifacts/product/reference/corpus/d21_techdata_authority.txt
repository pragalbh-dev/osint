**Jane's International Defence Review** — Asia-Pacific Air Power Watch
Issue dated June 2022 | Weekly briefing supplement

**Pakistan's HQ-9/P Long-Range SAM: Sustainment and Interoperability Notes Two Years After Induction**

Islamabad — Reporting from regional trade sources and satellite-imagery analysts continues to build a partial picture of the Pakistan Air Force's (PAF) HQ-9/P long-range surface-to-air missile battalions, first confirmed operational at bases in the Karachi and central Punjab air defence sectors in late 2021. The export variant, believed to be broadly analogous to the baseline HQ-9 developed by China Aerospace Science and Industry Corporation (CASIC), reportedly equips at least two, and possibly three, PAF air defence squadrons, though the exact battalion count remains unconfirmed by open sources.

Industry contacts describe the HQ-9/P battery as built around the HT-233 phased-array engagement radar (alternatively referenced in some Pakistani service literature simply as the "fire-control and guidance radar"), which performs target tracking, missile uplink and terminal guidance functions in a manner analysts continue to compare to the Russian 30N6E "Flap Lid" family associated with S-300PMU derivatives. Estimated engagement range for the HQ-9/P is put at approximately 100–125 km against aerodynamic targets, though at least one regional trade estimate has cited figures as high as 200 km for a purported extended-range variant — a figure this publication cannot independently verify and treats with caution.

Of particular interest to sustainment analysts is the software and calibration regime underpinning the HT-233 engagement radar. Multiple sources familiar with CASIC export practice, speaking on condition they not be further identified, indicate that radar mode libraries, waveform parameters and calibration tables are not locally modifiable by end-user technical staff; rather, updates and re-certification are said to be routed through a Chinese state technical-data authority, understood to sit within the broader CASIC/CPMIEC export administrative chain, which retains configuration control over the underlying software baseline. This arrangement is reportedly consistent with China's standard export posture for the HQ-9/FD-2000 family sold to Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan and other customers, and would imply that any deep software-level recalibration, electronic-counter-countermeasures (ECCM) tuning, or frequency-agility adjustment requires either the return of line-replaceable units to the OEM or the deployment of Chinese field-service representatives — a dependency PAF logisticians have not publicly addressed and which several analysts describe as a largely invisible constraint on wartime autonomy.

A retired PAF air defence officer, contacted informally, characterised local depot-level maintenance as limited to "organic" tasks — power supply units, cooling systems, mechanical traverse assemblies and cabling — while software-level diagnostics and radar signal-processing updates are said to require periodic contractor support visits, the frequency of which is not disclosed in available program documentation. It remains unclear whether Pakistan has negotiated source-code access or an indigenous calibration capability as part of the original contract, reportedly signed around 2019–2020; no open-source contract text has surfaced to confirm terms.

The missile itself is a two-stage design employing inertial mid-course guidance with datalink correction and semi-active/track-via-missile terminal homing, broadly consistent with published characterisations of the parent HQ-9 system. Warhead yield is generally estimated in the 180 kg class, though some regional handbooks list a figure closer to 200 kg; this publication was unable to reconcile the discrepancy.

PAF officials have not responded to queries regarding integration of the HQ-9/P engagement radar with the wider national Air Defence Ground Environment System (ADGES), though a senior official quoted last year in a domestic outlet said integration work was "ongoing" without further elaboration. Analysts caution that until independent confirmation emerges of a locally sovereign software-maintenance path, the system's effective operational tempo in a contested electromagnetic environment will likely remain contingent on continued technical engagement with Chinese suppliers — a point of concern raised, off the record, by at least two South Asian defence analysts interviewed for this report.

— compiled from staff and contributor reporting; additional details from regional correspondents in Islamabad and Beijing were unavailable for on-record confirmation at time of publication.

COAS VISITS ARMY AIR DEFENCE CENTRE, KARACHI

Rawalpindi – 14 October 2021: The Chief of Army Staff (COAS), General Qamar Javed Bajwa, today visited the Army Air Defence Centre, Karachi, where a new High to Medium Air Defence System (HIMADS), reportedly the HQ-9/P, was formally inducted into service with a Pakistan Army Air Defence (PAAD) unit.

On arrival, the COAS was received by the General Officer Commanding and senior officers of Army Air Defence. He was given a detailed briefing on the operational employment, technical parameters and integration of the newly commissioned system within the Command, Control, Communication and Intelligence Air Defence (CLIAD) network.

The system, believed to be a variant of the Chinese Hongqi-9 (HQ-9P/FD-2000 family) long-range surface-to-air missile, is understood to extend Pakistan's ground-based air defence envelope well beyond 100 km, providing improved coverage against high-value aerial threats including fighter aircraft, cruise missiles and other precision-guided munitions operating at medium-to-high altitude. Officials briefing the COAS stated that the system's engagement envelope and multi-target handling capability significantly enhance the layered air defence architecture guarding critical installations and population centres.

The exact number of batteries/launchers (TELs) inducted was not disclosed, in keeping with the Army's standing practice of not releasing order-of-battle details for sensitive air defence assets. An ISPR official, speaking on background, said only that "sufficient numbers" had been commissioned to meet operational requirements in the assigned area of responsibility, and that further inductions may follow in a phased manner.

The COAS, addressing the officers and troops of the newly raised/re-equipped unit, said that the induction of this state-of-the-art air defence system was a manifestation of the Army's continuous effort to modernize its air defence capability in line with the evolving threat spectrum. He said the system's induction reflects the enduring defence cooperation between Pakistan and China and reaffirmed that Army Air Defence remains fully prepared to defend Pakistani airspace against all forms of aerial aggression.

Later, the COAS also witnessed a technical demonstration and interacted with troops of the unit. He appreciated the professionalism of officers and men of Army Air Defence and lauded their efforts in achieving operational readiness of the newly commissioned system within a short span of time.

The visit concluded with the COAS reviewing a guard of honour and a group photograph with the officers of the unit.

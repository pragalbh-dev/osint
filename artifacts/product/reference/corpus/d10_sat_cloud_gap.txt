1. PURPOSE
Routine monitoring pass over previously identified air defense node in vicinity of a known PAF forward operating base, tasked to assess launcher disposition and site activity following reported unit rotation.

2. SUMMARY OF FINDINGS
Collection over the primary AOI on 10 May was severely degraded by persistent cumulus/stratocumulus cover, estimated at 90–95% scene obscuration across the central firing battery footprint. Only intermittent breaks along the northeastern access road and a portion of the perimeter berm were visible. As a result, analysts are unable to confirm the current count of transporter-erector-launchers (TELs) present at the site; prior reporting had indicated a battery-strength deployment of 6-8 launchers, but this figure cannot be verified against today's pass.

Partial imagery does show what is assessed with moderate confidence to be a HT-233 (H-200) engagement radar array, oriented on its habitual azimuth, and two support vehicles parked along the access spur nearest the maintenance shed. No launch canister activity was discernible in the visible fraction of the scene.

3. ANALYST ASSESSMENT
The site is believed to be an active HQ-9/P (export variant, reportedly designated FD-2000 in open marketing literature) long-range SAM position, part of a wider air defense belt reportedly covering approach corridors toward Sargodha and associated PAF installations. As of 10 May 2025, the battery is assessed to remain in a ready or near-ready posture, consistent with prior collects; however, given the near-total cloud obscuration, launcher count, reload status, and radar emission state could not be independently confirmed this cycle.

It should be noted that the HQ-9/P system associated with this site was reportedly first delivered to the Pakistan Air Force in 2021, with initial operational capability declared shortly thereafter — a data point that continues to inform the baseline order-of-battle estimate carried in this file. Analysts caution that no fresh confirmation of this delivery timeline has been obtained since that period, and the figure is retained here as background context rather than a current observation.

Estimated engagement envelope for the HQ-9/P remains consistent with open-source figures (max intercept range on the order of 200-260km depending on missile variant/round mix), though the specific missile loadout at this site — whether standard HQ-9 rounds or extended-range variants — could not be assessed from available imagery.

4. LIMITATIONS
- Scene obscuration: ~95% cloud cover over primary target area; unable to confirm launcher count or emplacement status.
- No SAR or complementary sensor tasked for this pass; next EO opportunity dependent on forecast clearing, tentatively NLT 14 May.
- Radar activity (RF/ELINT correlation) not available for this report; assessment is imagery-only.
- Vehicle count/type ID limited to the small unobscured fraction (~5-10%) of the site.

5. RECOMMENDATION
Re-task next available clear-weather pass, prioritize SAR collection to bridge cloud gap, and cross-check against SIGINT reporting for radar emission activity in the interim period.
